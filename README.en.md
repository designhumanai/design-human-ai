## ğŸ”· What is DHAIE?

**DHAIE** (Design Human AI Engineering and Enhancement) is a research initiative developing philosophical and practical foundations for the next generation of human-machine interfaces.

### Core Idea

Traditional "human-centered AI" assumes that designers can occupy the position of external observers, designing solutions "for users". DHAIE proceeds from **second-order cybernetics**: the designer is inevitably part of the observed system, and **the design process itself** is an act of reality construction.

**We don't design *for* users. We create conditions for the mutual becoming of humans and AI.**

### What Makes DHAIE Unique

- **Microstrategies of Cognition** â€“ formalization of individual thinking patterns, independent of content
- **Assemblage Theory** â€“ humans and AI don't exchange information, but rather mutually constitute each other
- **Reflexive Interfaces** â€“ systems capable of observing and modifying their own logic
- **Positive Spirals** â€“ mechanisms of sustainable mutual enrichment of capabilities

## ğŸ” DHAIE's Philosophical Position

### Comparison of Approaches to AGI

| Parameter | Skeptical Position | AGI Industry | DHAIE |
|----------|---------------------|---------------|-------|
| **AGI as...** | Philosophically impossible subject | Engineering task | Co-evolutionary interface |
| **Key Limitation** | Infinite choice barrier | Generalization and metacognition | Context incoherence |
| **Human's Role** | Source of meaning (AGI is autonomous) | Source of data and verification | Center of meaning assembly |
| **Conclusion** | AGI is fundamentally impossible | AGI is possible as engineering system | AGI is not self-sufficient â€“ symbiosis is key |

**DHAIE's Position**: We don't strive to create autonomous AGI. We design conditions for **mutual becoming** of humans and AI, where each one's capabilities amplify the other's.

*Table reflects the project's authorial position.*
