## ğŸ”· What is DHAIE?

**DHAIE** (Design Human AI Engineering and Enhancement) is a research initiative developing philosophical and practical foundations for the next generation of human-machine interfaces.

### Core Idea

Traditional "human-centered AI" assumes that designers can occupy an external observer position, creating solutions "for users". DHAIE is grounded in **second-order cybernetics**: the designer is inevitably part of the observed system, and the design process itself is an act of reality construction.

**We don't design for users. We create conditions for the mutual becoming of humans and AI.**

### What Distinguishes DHAIE

- **Microstrategies of cognition** â€” formalization of individual thinking patterns, independent of content
- **Assemblage theory** â€” humans and AI don't exchange information; they mutually constitute each other
- **Reflexive interfaces** â€” systems capable of observing and modifying their own logic
- **Positive spirals** â€” mechanisms for sustainable mutual enhancement of capabilities

### Comparing Approaches to AGI

| Parameter | Skeptical Position | AGI Industry | DHAIE |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|-------|
| **AGI asâ€¦** | Philosophically impossible subject | Engineering challenge | Co-evolutionary interface |
| **Key limitation** | Infinite choice barrier | Generalization & meta-cognition | Context misalignment |
| **Human role** | Source of meaning (AGI is autonomous) | Data source & validator | Center of meaning assembly |
| **Conclusion** | AGI is fundamentally impossible | AGI is achievable as engineered system | AGI is not the goal â€” symbiosis matters |

**DHAIE's Position**: We don't aim to create autonomous AGI. We design conditions for the **mutual becoming** of humans and AI, where each enhances the capabilities of the other.
